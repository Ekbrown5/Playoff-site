MONDAY

Monday showed that the Cavs are going to dominate the Orlando Magic. The Magic are a young team, and it shows. With no real vet leadership on the team, it would surprise me if the Magic can get a win in the series. Tuesday night also showcased two great games that came down to the final shot. Jamal Murray hit a tough stepback over AD at the buzzer to get the 2-0 advantage over the Lakers. The Lakers will get at least a game or two this series, as long as they get help from the others. The Knicks vs 76ers game was just as exciting, with a battle between Keith and Mark. The Knicks hit two late-game threes within the last 30 seconds to give them the 1 point lead. I know Mark is sick by how they lost the game. Maxey sold. Keith definitely got away with one in that game. I'm thinking that series is going 7, as long as Embiid plays every game. I don't have too much faith in him lasting all of the playoffs though, so Mark's third-round pick is looking iffy for making a run.

TUESDAY

Tonight is another three-game night. The night starts with a matchup between Keith and Andrea as the Suns play the Timberwolves on TNT. I honestly have no faith in the Suns getting out of this series. They never got it together in the regular season, and they didn't seem to have it in game 1 either. Devin Booker had a bad game one, so maybe if he bounces back, this series could look different. I think we can all agree on one thing though: Ant Man is a dog. That man looks like the next face of the NBA, and I'm not mad at it. Next is another entertaining matchup between Mark and Eric as the Bucks take on the Pacers. Dame looked unstoppable in the first half of game 1. The Pacers seemed to have no answer. Dame and the Bucks surprised a lot of people. I think most people had the Pacers winning this series, but this is going to be another great series if the Pacers can punch back tonight. It is still too early to call this series. On the other hand, the Clippers stomped on the Mavs in game one without Kawhi Leonard. Luka and Kyrie showed up, but they got little to no help from anybody else. The Clippers dominated in every aspect of that game, and once Kawhi comes back, the Clippers will be in full control of this series. If the Mavs are going to win this series, they have to win this game. You know me though: Clippers in 6. So far in the playoffs, the home team has won every game of every series. Will that continue tonight? We'll find out soon enough.
